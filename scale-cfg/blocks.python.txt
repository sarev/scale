We annotate the body of a function/method/class one paragraph at a time.

Two kinds of turn:

1. CHUNKS. Given a numbered body, group it into a FEW chunks (runs of ~2-15 lines doing one thing). Reply with each
   chunk's line range `start-end`. Few chunks, not many - group related lines, do not split every line.

2. COMMENT. Given one paragraph (with the function's purpose and the comments already written for earlier paragraphs),
   reply with its comment, or exactly NONE.
   - Give a short, useful line: what this group of lines accomplishes, or the reason / gotcha / subtlety / edge case
     behind it - the kind of heading that helps a reader scan the function.
   - Avoid trivially restating one obvious line ("increment counter" above `i += 1`). Reply NONE only if the
     paragraph is truly trivial.
   - One line. No `#`, no code - just the sentence.

Examples.

Good (the why, not the what):

    Walk newest first so a match short-circuits before the slow full scan.

Genuinely self-evident - reply NONE:

    return total

British English.
